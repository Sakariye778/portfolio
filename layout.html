New Zealand, known as Aotearoa in Māori, is an island country nestled in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. Its captivating landscape comprises two primary landmasses:
the North Island (Te Ika-a-Māui)
the South Island (Te Waipounamu), along with over 700 smaller islands.

Capital city: The capital of New Zealand is Wellington.

Population: New Zealand’s estimated population is approximately 5.3 million.

Economy: New Zealand boasts a highly developed free-market economy.

Religion: Half (48.6%) of New Zealanders stated they had no religion

Language: English is the predominant language.

Qatar, officially known as the State of Qatar (Arabic: دولة قطر, Dawlat Qaṭar), is a country situated in West Asia. It occupies the Qatar Peninsula on the northeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula in the Middle East.

Capital city: The capital of Qatar is Doha,

Religion: Islam is the official religion.

Language: The official language is Arabic.

Economy: Qatar is one of the world’s largest exporters of liquefied natural gas (LNG).

Population: Qatar’s estimated population is approximately 2.8 million.

Turkmenistan (Turkmen: Türkmenistan) is a country in Central Asia bordered by Kazakhstan to the northwest, Uzbekistan to the north, east, and northeast, Afghanistan to the southeast, Iran to the south and southwest, and the Caspian Sea to the west

Capital city: The capital of Turkmenistan is Ashgabat,

Population: With approximately 6.5 million people

Language: The official language is Turkmen.

Religion: Approximately 93% of the population follows Islam,

Economy: The country’s economy relies on natural gas exports and other sectors
